A few days ago I stumbled onto Bear Blog, and its idea and philosophy nudged me to revisit my own blog. It feels like a small act of resistance in the best way. The idea is simple: keep the web lightweight, keep the focus on the words, and let pages load instantly everywhere. There's no bloat, no feed games, no complicated stacks to keep alive. Just writing, delivered as plain, fast pages. That clarity is what made me notice it in the first place, and it's what keeps me coming back.

And that philosophy feels even more relevant right now. The modern web is powerful, but it's also noisy: trackers, popups, autoplay, cookie walls, and megabytes of JavaScript just to read a paragraph. All that weight costs time, battery, and attention. It also raises real barriers for people on slow connections or older devices. Bear Blog is a reminder that we can choose a different path and still build something beautiful.

What I love most is the quiet confidence in the approach: the writing matters, not the platform. A minimal page respects the reader's time and keeps the author's focus on the ideas. It also makes publishing less fragile. Fewer dependencies means fewer surprises, fewer updates, and fewer moments where a post won't load because some script failed. It's the kind of simple infrastructure that helps you show up and write.

This blog runs on a theme derived from Bear Blog, which is the real reason for this post. I wanted a setup that felt calm and intentional, and the Bear-inspired theme hit that sweet spot. It stays out of the way, lets the text breathe, and keeps the whole site tiny. I didn't want a flashy portfolio; I wanted a notebook that loads quickly and invites you to read.

So here's a small thank-you to Bear Blog for the inspiration.
